Historical Fiction Books

Explore each book further by clicking on the covers!

The Book Thief

Author: Markus Zusak

Summary: Set in Nazi Germany, this is a powerful story of a young girl who steals books and shares them with her neighbors, as narrated by Death. It's a tale of love, resilience, and the impact of stories in dark times.

Publication Year: 2005

All the Light We Cannot See

Author: Anthony Doerr

Summary: A beautifully woven tale about a blind French girl and a German soldier whose lives intersect during World War II, highlighting themes of courage, survival, and the human spirit.

Publication Year: 2014

The Nightingale

Author: Kristin Hannah

Summary: A gripping story of two sisters in Nazi-occupied France during World War II, each facing unimaginable struggles and risks to resist and survive the brutality of war.

Publication Year: 2015

The Other Boleyn Girl

Author: Philippa Gregory

Summary: A historical drama following Mary Boleyn and her sister Anne as they navigate the treacherous court of King Henry VIII, marked by intrigue, rivalry, and ambition.

Publication Year: 2001

Beneath a Scarlet Sky

Author: Mark T. Sullivan

Summary: Based on a true story, this is a tale of an Italian teenager who helps Jews escape Nazi capture, serving as a spy within the German command, showcasing heroism amidst the horrors of WWII.

Publication Year: 2017

The Pillars of the Earth

Author: Ken Follett

Summary: Set in 12th-century England, this epic novel chronicles the lives of various characters entangled in the construction of a cathedral, filled with ambition, betrayal, and devotion.

Publication Year: 1989

Girl with a Pearl Earring

Author: Tracy Chevalier

Summary: Inspired by the famous painting, this novel tells the story of a young maid who becomes a muse for the artist Vermeer, delving into art, desire, and the intricacies of Dutch society.

Publication Year: 1999

Atonement

Author: Ian McEwan

Summary: A tale of love and tragedy spanning from pre-WWII England to the 21st century, this novel explores guilt, redemption, and the consequences of one fateful decision.

Publication Year: 2001

The Paris Architect

Author: Charles Belfoure

Summary: In Nazi-occupied Paris, an architect is recruited to design hiding spaces for Jews. What begins as a business opportunity turns into a moral journey of courage and compassion.

Publication Year: 2013

The Secret Keeper

Author: Kate Morton

Summary: A suspenseful tale of family secrets and mysteries spanning decades, following a daughter’s journey to uncover her mother's hidden past during WWII.

Publication Year: 2012
